Conquering Chaos: Basic Home Management

Keeping a house organized and running smoothly can feel overwhelming at times. But don't worry, even small steps can make a big difference! This section offers some tips for basic home management to help you create a more peaceful and organized environment.

  • Tame the clutter: Dedicate 10-15 minutes daily to decluttering a specific area. Donate or discard unused items.
  • Embrace the power of lists: Make to-do lists for daily chores, grocery shopping, or errands. It helps prioritize tasks and avoid feeling overwhelmed.
  • Schedule cleaning routines: Divide cleaning tasks into daily, weekly, and monthly schedules. This creates a manageable routine and prevents last-minute cleaning sprees.
  • Meal planning is key: Plan your meals for the week to avoid unhealthy takeout or impulse grocery purchases. Utilize leftovers creatively!
  • Delegate and involve: Encourage family members to help with chores according to their age and abilities. It fosters teamwork and responsibility.


Spark Joy: The KonMari Method for Decluttering

Step 1: Commit to Tidying Up

The first step is to make a firm decision to declutter your home. Visualize the joy of a more organized space and commit to the process.

Step 2: Tidy by Category, Not Location

Forget cleaning room by room! Instead, focus on categories like clothes, books, papers, miscellaneous items (komono), and sentimental items. This helps you make consistent decisions based on the "spark joy" principle.

Step 3: Take Everything Out

Gather all items in a category from your entire home and put them in one central location. Seeing the volume you possess helps you make informed decisions.

Step 4: Does It Spark Joy?

Pick up each item and hold it. Ask yourself: "Does this spark joy?". If yes, keep it. If not, thank the item and discard it. Be honest and let go of things that don't bring joy.

Step 5: Thank The Items That Don't Spark Joy

Expressing gratitude to objects you're letting go of acknowledges their usefulness and promotes a more mindful approach to decluttering.

Step 6: Discard and Organize

Discard the items that don't spark joy. Organize the remaining ones in a way that makes them easily accessible and visually pleasing. The KonMari Method suggests specific folding techniques for clothes.
